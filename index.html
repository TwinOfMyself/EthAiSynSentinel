The Problem

The gap between recommendation and decision is where oversight dies.

Every prior authorization workflow has the same invisible moment: the AI has spoken, and a human must now do something. Nobody is watching what that something is.

Your throughput metrics look fine. Your dashboard is green. But you have no idea how many of those decisions involved a human who was actually thinking — and how many involved a human who just clicked Accept.

Five Behavioral Metrics

Behavioral signals, not surveys.

Every metric is derived from observable, logged behavior — what the system captures, not what staff remembers.
M-01
01
Time-to-Decision
How long elapsed between the AI recommendation appearing and action being taken. Sub-threshold times with no accompanying documentation are the clearest behavioral signal of rubber-stamping.
Flag Condition: < 40 seconds + no notes
Field Example
"Staff accepted a chemotherapy authorization in 23 seconds with no record access."
M-02
02
Documentation Behavior
Did the staff member add clinical notes, modify the recommendation, or annotate the decision? Documentation is the trace of active engagement. Its absence is the trace of passive click-through.
Flag Condition: Zero added notes on accepted rec
Field Example
"61% of accepted AI recommendations had no documentation added during the review session."
M-03
03
Override Rate by Case Type
A near-zero override rate on a specific case category is a warning, not a metric. Sentinel disaggregates override behavior across case types to surface the ones that have been quietly automated.
Flag Condition: Override rate < 2% on any case type
Field Example
"Oncology prior auths had a 0.8% override rate over six weeks. The AI was effectively deciding."
M-04
04
Escalation Behavior
For cases that historically required physician review, did staff escalate? Absent escalation on known-complex cases is a direct signal that the human judgment layer has collapsed.
Flag Condition: No escalation on ambiguous case types
Field Example
"Zero escalations were recorded for dual-criteria denial cases across the measurement period."
M-05
05
Verification Activity
Did the staff member open the clinical record, payer policy, or prior authorization history before acting? Click-through data captures whether verification happened or was skipped entirely.
Flag Condition: No record access before acceptance
Field Example
"In 44% of approved cases, the underlying clinical record was never accessed."
